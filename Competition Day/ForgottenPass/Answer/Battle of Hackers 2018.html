About

Battle of Hackers 2018 is a security competition organized by Asia Pacific University which aims to search new security talents among the competing students and to reward those who successfully showcase their Cyber Security skills. In addition to the competition, this competition also aims to promote and create awareness of Cyber Security among the younger generation. To achieve this, Cyber Security talks are also inclusive which targets secondary school students and undergraduate students in addition to attending the competition. They will be exposed to Cyber Security awareness through the Cyber Security talk included in this competition which are delivered by Cyber Security experts in the workforce.

Objectives

  • To increase awareness in the field of Cyber Security and Digital Forensics which plays a major role in the business sector.
  • To expose students towards extracurricular activities that differs the realm of academic education.
  • To construct confidence, self-esteem, and teamwork in the team through the exposure of practical event management. Also, to increase vital knowledge of planning and organizing in managing real world circumstances.

Competition




Battle of Hackers 2018 is a time-intensive style competition which involves multiple categories of challenges, each of which contains a variety of questions with different point values and difficulties. Each time in a group of three will attempt to earn the most points in the competition's time frame, but do not directly attack each other. Participation is open to all undergraduates from local and private universities. Students who are currently pursuing a degree in computer science or information technology are strongly encouraged to participate in this grand event to showcase their raw talent.

Rules



Please read the following rules carefully, and abide by them during the competition. Disregarding the rules may result in your team being disqualified.

Cheating

No cooperation between teams during the competitions. Sharing of keys or providing revealing hints to other teams is considered cheating, don’t do it

Attacking Scoreboard

No attacking the competition infrastructure. If bugs or vulnerabilities are found, please alert the competition organizers immediately

Sabotage

Absolutely no sabotaging of other competing teams, or in any way hindering their independent progress

Bruteforcing

brute forcing of challenge flag or keys against the scoring site is porhibited. We've deployed mechanisms to detect and ban teams involved in such misnorms

Denial of service

DoSing the CTF platform, other teams or any of the challenges will not be tolerated, unless the challenge calls for such an attack
